# PatchLab documentation licensing

PatchLab documentation uses two licenses so teaching material remains reusable while
the executable tooling and product-derived fixtures stay aligned with PatchLab.

## Documentation and media

Unless a file says otherwise, prose, diagrams, and media under `src/content/docs/`,
`src/assets/`, and `project/` are licensed under the
[Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International license](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/)
(`CC-BY-SA-4.0`). Attribute the work to “PatchLab contributors,” link to the source
repository, identify material changes, and share adaptations under the same license.

## Software and PatchLab fixtures

Source code, scripts, configuration, interactive components, generated registry data,
and downloadable `.plpatch` examples are licensed under the
[GNU General Public License version 3](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.html)
(`GPL-3.0-only`), matching the PatchLab product repository.

## Contributions

By contributing, you license your contribution under the license that applies to the
destination path. You retain your copyright. Third-party material must retain its own
notice and must be compatible with the destination license.

The SPDX identifiers above are normative. This summary is explanatory and does not
replace either license's legal text.
