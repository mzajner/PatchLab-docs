# Content standard

## Page contract

Every published page has:

- one user question or outcome;
- a unique title and descriptive summary;
- stated prerequisites when needed;
- exact UI labels and units;
- expected result and verification for procedures;
- safety notes close to the risky action;
- links to canonical concepts/reference instead of duplicated explanation; and
- no claims about planned behavior written in present tense.

## Language

- Lead with the outcome or model.
- Prefer concrete verbs: connect, measure, reduce, save.
- Define a technical term at first meaningful use.
- Use **you** for actions and the block/system name for behavior.
- Reserve **will** for deterministic outcomes under stated conditions.
- Distinguish listening impressions from measured facts.
- State sample rate, block size, channel layout, signal, and tolerance when they affect
  a quantitative result.

## DSP claims

For every substantive DSP claim, identify its class:

- Definition: support with a primary technical source.
- Product implementation: support with source and a product measurement.
- Rule of thumb: label it as approximate and state its domain.
- Listening guidance: name the source and level-matching conditions.

Never call a filter “analog,” a limiter “brickwall,” a process “transparent,” or an
output “alias-free” without defining the measurable meaning and operating conditions.

## Procedures

Use this sequence when applicable:

1. Goal
2. Before you begin
3. Safety
4. Steps
5. Expected result
6. Verify
7. If it differs
8. Extend

Do not mix required steps with optional theory in the same numbered list.

## Visuals

- A diagram explains relationships; a screenshot teaches recognition; a video teaches
  motion.
- Crop to the relevant surface, but retain enough context to orient the reader.
- Never use color alone to identify a cable or state.
- Keep annotations outside critical UI text where possible.
- Alt text describes the instructional information, not every decorative detail.

## Cross-references

Link the first point where a prerequisite becomes necessary. Use descriptive link text.
Do not add a generic “related topics” list unless the relationship is curated and
useful.

## Generated pages

Generated pages state provenance and remain terse. Editorial advice belongs in guides,
concepts, or structured annotations—not in hand-edited generated files.
